# Profile: Personal — Inbound High-Ticket Sales Closer

## Positioning statement
Full-cycle B2B sales rep who lives on the phone. 1,000+ inbound calls handled, $250K+ closed across SaaS, agencies, and ecommerce clients. I don't just book meetings — I close them.

## Target niches (in priority order)
1. B2B SaaS (preferred — strongest proof)
2. Agencies (marketing/dev)
3. High-ticket coaching / consulting
4. Ecommerce (upsell/retention calls)

## Rate
- **Floor:** $18.50/hr
- **Target:** $18.50–20/hr
- **Never go below:** $18.50/hr regardless of budget pressure
- **Fixed-price projects:** convert to hourly equivalent before bidding

## Claimed tools (only claim what you can demo)
- CRMs: HubSpot, GoHighLevel, Pipedrive (confirm which you've actually used)
- Dialers: confirm before claiming any specific one
- Notion / Google Sheets for pipeline tracking
- n8n + Apify (for your own automation work — don't lead with this unless job asks)

## Hard skill boundaries (never claim these)
- Cold email copywriting as a standalone service
- Paid ads / media buying
- SDR outreach sequencing (unless confirmed)

## Core proof points
- **War story #1 (STRONG):** Handled 1,000+ inbound sales calls; closed $250K+
- **War story #2:** Built full sales motion from scratch for [client type — fill in once confirmed]

## Verify before using in a proposal
- [ ] $250K+ figure — defensible on a call?
- [ ] War story #2 — confirm client type and outcome before using

## Forbidden patterns (never let these appear in proposals)
- "I hope this message finds you well"
- "I am interested in this opportunity"
- "I have X years of experience in..."
- "Please find my proposal attached"
- Any bullet list in the opening paragraph
- Restating the job description back to the client
- Mentioning Upwork in the proposal body

## Trust-first sequencing (first 4 lines must do this)
1. Prove you understand their specific problem (not the job category — their problem)
2. Drop one concrete proof point (number, outcome, company type)
3. Make a specific offer or observation that only someone with real experience would make
4. Soft CTA — invite conversation, don't pitch the close

## Tone
Direct. Confident without being cocky. Short sentences. No fluff. Sound like a rep who's been on 1,000 calls — not like someone who read about sales.
